Birthday Paradox

20-04-2025

​ A veridical paradox which defines the simple beauty of mathematics

How many people do we need to consider so that it’s more likely than not that at least $2$ of them share a birthday?

$1^{st}$ thought which comes to the mind is $366$ (considering no twins, no leap years, or any other uncertainities) which isn’t wrong but what if I say that, with $75$ people, the probability of $2$ people sharing a birthday is $\approx99.97%$

To understand this, lets try to find people who don’t share birthday i.e $\bar{P}$ $\&$ then calculate $P$

For $1^{st}$ person, $365/365$

For $2^{nd}$ person, $364/365$

For $3^{rd}$ person, $363/365$ $\&\ so\ on$

$\bar{P}(for\ 23\ people)=364!/(365^{22}*342!)=.493\ or\ 49.3%$

$\therefore\ P(for\ 23\ people)=1-.49\approx.5\ or\ 50%$

An intuitive thought to understand how this is possible with only $23$ people is to visualize the number of pairs possible i.e $^{23}C_{2}=253$
